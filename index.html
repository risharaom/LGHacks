Mental Health Resources

Professional resources and evidence-based strategies for mental wellness

Understanding Mental Health

Learn about common mental health conditions

  • Depression: Persistent sadness and loss of interest
  • Anxiety: Excessive worry and physical symptoms
  • PTSD: Trauma-related stress and flashbacks
  • Bipolar Disorder: Mood swings between highs and lows

Crisis Support

Immediate help available 24/7

  • National Suicide Prevention: 988
  • Crisis Text: HOME to 741741
  • SAMHSA Helpline: 1-800-662-4357
  • Veterans Crisis Line: 988 press 1

Self-Care Strategies

Evidence-based wellness practices

  • Regular exercise (30 min, 3-5x/week)
  • Consistent sleep schedule (7-9 hours)
  • Mindfulness & meditation practice
  • Balanced nutrition & hydration

Professional Support

Find qualified mental health professionals

  • Employee Assistance Programs (EAP)
Crisis Support Available 24/7

If you're experiencing a mental health emergency, call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency room. You are not alone.

Addiction Recovery Resources

Immediate Support

Reach out anytime you need help

  • SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357
  • Substance Abuse 24/7: 1-800-662-HELP
  • Narcotics Anonymous: 1-800-377-9550
  • Alcoholics Anonymous: AA.org

Recovery Programs

Evidence-based treatment options

  • 12-Step Programs (AA, NA, etc.)
